## 5. Mars Clicker — Unity Analytics

**Mars Clicker** uses Unity Analytics (Unity Developer Data Framework) to collect
anonymous gameplay data. This section applies exclusively to Mars Clicker,
available on Steam, Epic Games Store, and Microsoft Store / Xbox.

### Data collected

Unity Analytics collects the following data automatically:

- **Unique installation-specific ID** — an anonymous identifier tied to the
  installation, not to any personal identity.
- **Gameplay events** — sessions, in-game interactions, and progression data.
- **Software and hardware diagnostics** — performance and stability information
  (OS version, device model, crash reports) used solely to improve the game.

No name, email address, or any directly identifying information is collected.

### Legal framework

Under GDPR, Unity acts as the Processor and Adrián Corbalán acts as the
Controller. Under CCPA (as amended by CPRA), Unity acts as the Service Provider
and Adrián Corbalán acts as the Business.

### User consent

As the developer and data controller, Adrián Corbalán is responsible for
providing clear and prominent notices to users regarding the collection,
use, and transfer of their data, and for obtaining any necessary consent.

Mars Clicker implements the `UnityConsent` API to manage analytics consent.
Analytics data is only collected when the user grants consent.

### Data retention

Personal data collected via Unity Analytics is retained for a default period
of 30 days.

### Data subject requests (access & deletion)

To request access to or deletion of your data, contact Unity directly at
[dpo@unity3d.com](mailto:dpo@unity3d.com), or reach out via the contact
details in Section 8 of this policy.

### Unity's privacy policy

For full details on how Unity handles data, refer to the
[Unity Game Player and App User Privacy Policy](https://unity.com/legal/game-player-and-app-user-privacy-policy).
